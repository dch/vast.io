A Scalable Platform for Network Forensics

Features

Interactive. VAST is designed to answer complex queries in sub-second latencies, allowing incident responders and threat hunters to quickly achieve their tasks with maximum productivity.

Scalable. VAST harnesses all available CPU cores in your machine and transparently scales to cluster of commodity machines. To this end, VAST leverages the C++ Actor Framework as high-performance runtime.

Expressive. VAST offers a rich type system to describe your data, giving you flexible query operations (e.g., IP address prefix search, set membership) and unleashing type-specific internal optimizations.

Community

While we are developing VAST, we want to stay in touch with you and seek an active exchange. After all, you will be using it and we are working hard to deliver you a satisfying solution. These are the various channels to reach us:

Chat. We listen. If you have direct questions for the developers of VAST, join our Gitter chat. As die-hard C++ fans, we also have dedicated C++ channel.

GitHub. We share our work. VAST ships with a permissive 3-clause BSD license. If you have trouble with the code, please do not hesitate to file an issue at our tracker.

Twitter. We spread the word. Follow @vast_io where we will periodically post noteworthy tidbits about VAST.

Mailing Lists. We listen to you. To reach the community, send an email to our mailing list. Our commit diffs ship to a separate mailing list. And if you can't find answer anywhere, you can always send an email to info@vast.io.

About

Academic Origin

The abstract idea of VAST manifested in 2008 as a research paper about Principles for Developing Comprehensive Network Visibility by Mark Allman (who coined the term VAST), Christian Kreibich, Vern Paxson, Robin Sommer, and Nicholas Weaver.

Funding

The Signatures Innovation Fellows Program currently funds VAST development and research. This program supports innovative research from UC Berkeley faculty that hold commercial promise. We are grateful for their support.
